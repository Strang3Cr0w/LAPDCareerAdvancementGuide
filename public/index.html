LAPD Career Advancement Guide

The LAPD Career Advancement Guide is a comprehensive roadmap for officers to excel in their careers. With valuable insights and expert tips, this guide is the ultimate resource for any aspiring or current LAPD officer looking to move up in their career.

Mindset and Skills

Effective leadership requires a unique set of skills. Learn how developing the right mindset and skills can set you apart from the rest. Leadership can be learned and honed, so start your development today.

Commitment

To be a great leader, you must be fully committed to your profession. Recognize the importance of what you do and take ownership of your duties.

Empathy

Leadership goes beyond the badge. Be part of the community and put people first, especially when they are most vulnerable.

Humility

A good leader is self-aware and open to feedback. Recognize that you don't know everything and be willing to learn from others.

Communication

A strong communicator can convey their message effectively. Listen actively and communicate clearly for maximum impact.

Leadership Styles

Leadership styles can vary based on individual personality, situational factors, and personal beliefs. Learn about the most common leadership styles and how they can influence your approach to leadership.


Directive Leadership

This style is characterized by a top-down approach, where officers are expected to follow strict rules and guidelines. This style works well in high-risk situations.

Delegative Leadership

This style allows officers to take on more responsibility and have more control over their work. This style works well in supporting community programs and initiatives.

Participative Leadership

This style encourages officers to share their thoughts and ideas, resulting in increased morale, innovation, and collaboration in decision-making.
